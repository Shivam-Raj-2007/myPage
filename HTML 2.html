Acid,Baces And Salt(Description List)




(1)Acides :--
A substance with particular chemical properties including turning litmus red, neutralizing alkalis, and dissolving some metals; typically, a corrosive or sour-tasting liquid of this kind . Example:--
(i)Acetic Acid
(ii)Formic Acid
(iii)Citric Acid
(iv)Lactic Acid
(v)Oxalic Acid
(vi)Ascordic Acid (Vitamin C)
(vii)Tataric Acid

(2)Baces :--
Any substance that in water solution is slippery to the touch, tastes bitter, changes the colour of indicators , reacts with acids to form salts, and promotes certain chemical reactions (base catalysis). Example:--
(i)Calcium hydroxide
(ii)Ammonium hydroxide
(iii)Sodium hydroxide / Potassium hydroxide
(iv)Magnesium hydroxide

(3)Salt :--
Any chemical compound formed from the reaction of an acid with a base, with all or part of the hydrogen of the acid replaced by a metal or other cation.

(4)Litmus Paper :--
Paper stained with litmus which is used to indicate the acidity or alkalinity of a substance. Blue litmus paper turns red under acid conditions, and red litmus paper turns blue under alkaline conditions. Example:--
(i)Red Litmus Paper
(ii)Blue Litmus Paper
(iii)Turmeric
(iv)China Rose
(v)phenolphthalein

(5)Neutralisation:--
The reaction between an acid and a base is known as a neutralisation . Salt and water are produced in this process with the evolution of heat. Reaction :--
(i)Acid + Base --> Salt + Water
(ii)Hydrochloric acid(HCL)+Sodium hydroxide(NaOH)--> Sodium cloride(NaCL) + Water(H2O)

(6)Neutralisation In Everyday Life :--
It helps us to digest food. But too much of acid in the stomach causes indigestion.To relieve indigestion, we take an antacid such as milk of magnesia, which contains magnesium hydroxide. It neutralises the effect of excessive acid . Example:--
(i)Indigestion
(ii)Ant Bite
(iii)Soil Treatment
(iv)Factory Wastes
